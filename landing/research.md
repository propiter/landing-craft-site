## 1. Product & Idea Validation

### What Landing Craft actually is
A **concierge service** (human-operated by propiter) that builds high-conversion, multi-page Next.js landings for founders, indie hackers, and small agencies. The client describes their product; propiter runs real market research, then builds a unique, alive, scroll-reactive landing — deployed, with working forms/analytics/SEO, and the client owns the code. No lock-in, no proprietary editor.

### The offer
- **"Get your first landing FREE"** — hosted on `*.vercel.app` (free domain)
- Paid plans for: edits, additional landings, custom domain support
- Maker brand: **propiter** (github.com/propiter) — personal, indie, high-trust

### Idea validation — CREDIBLE ✅
The market for "AI-generated websites" is saturated, but there is a **real, vocal frustration** with the output quality of AI builders (generic designs, broken on deploy, lock-in). A human-operated service that delivers **real code, real research, and real taste** fills a genuine gap. The "first landing free" offer removes the primary risk for skeptical buyers who've been burned by AI tools.

### Category positioning
Landing Craft is NOT an "AI landing page builder." It's positioned as a **concierge landing service** — a category **between** AI self-serve tools (Lovable, v0, Bolt) and traditional agencies ($5K–$50K). This is a defensible category.

## 6. Validation — Evidence That Supports Our Claims

### 6.1 "AI builders produce generic output"
- **Product Hunt Lovable reviews (189 reviews):** Repeated complaints about "buggy," "complexity handling issues," "limited prompts." One reviewer: "v0 is great for UI but stops at the front-end." Another: "it rewrites the entire file, breaks UI/UX structure, and still fails to fix the original problem."
- **Bolt PH reviews (44 reviews):** "Bolt rewrites excessively just to consume more tokens." "Same story as with most of these AI tools." "Images were broken or showed the wrong car."
- **The "shadcn default" problem:** v0's output all looks like shadcn/ui. It's become the new Bootstrap — recognizable, generic, the "AI look" everyone's trying to escape.

### 6.2 "Most tools give you a mockup that breaks on deploy"
- **Lovable reviews:** "Sometimes the platform reverts some unwanted changes when you are trying to change something else." Bug fixing consumes credits. Forms and analytics often non-functional.
- **Bolt reviews:** "When you ask Bolt to fix a simple bug, it often rewrites the entire file, breaks your UI/UX structure."
- **Durable explicitly:** "We don't offer extensive options for HTML customization." — You literally CAN'T fix things.

### 6.3 "Lock-in is real and people hate it"
- **Framer:** Proprietary platform. Can't export to standard React/Next.js. You leave Framer, you lose your site.
- **Durable:** domain lock-in, can't edit code, can't transfer easily ("it takes a couple of weeks to transfer over and has a minimum subscription time").
- **Lovable:** While you can export to GitHub, the AI-generated code is often messy and hard to maintain without Lovable as the "CTO."
- **Webflow/Framer:** No-code lock-in. The design IS the platform.

### 6.4 Success of human-operated services vs AI
- **The concierge model works:** services like DesignJoy (design-as-a-subscription), Copy.ai's human+AI hybrid, and traditional agencies all thrive because people will pay for **taste, reliability, and accountability** that pure AI can't deliver.
- **Maker-led brands win trust:** propiter (personal GitHub brand) has more credibility than a faceless AI company. Indie hackers trust other indie hackers.
- **"First one free" has historical precedent:** It's the classic SaaS acquisition model (Calendly, Loom, Notion). Lowers the barrier to trust.
