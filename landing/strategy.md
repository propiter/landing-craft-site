## 5. Messaging Pillars

Every message hangs on one of these four. Nothing goes out that doesn't serve at least one.

### Pillar 1: Researched, not templated
We study your market, your competitors, and your audience BEFORE we open a code editor. No AI auto-completes your positioning. The result is a landing that feels like it was BUILT for your product — not filled in from a template.

### Pillar 2: Real code you own
You get a Next.js site in your own GitHub repo. No proprietary editor, no platform lock-in, no "export?" button that doesn't actually work. If you hire a dev next month, they know exactly what they're looking at.

### Pillar 3: Deployed and working — not a mockup
Forms send emails. Analytics fire. SEO tags are real, not placeholders. Your landing is live on a URL you can share right now — not a preview behind a login wall.

### Pillar 4: Human taste. Human accountability.
A real person (propiter) builds every landing. If something's off, you talk to a human who fixes it — not a prompt box that burns credits producing the same broken output. Taste is the moat. Accountability is the promise.

## 7. Voice & Tone

Informed by `brand-voice` skill: direct, anti-slop, human, warm but professional, founder-to-founder.

### Voice attributes
| Attribute | Sounds like | Does NOT sound like |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Direct** | "You describe your product. I build your landing." | "Our innovative platform empowers you to..." |
| **Anti-slop** | "Real code. Not a template." | "Cutting-edge AI-powered solutions" |
| **Human** | "I research your market first. Then I design." | "Our engine processes your inputs" |
| **Founder-to-founder** | "I've been there. AI builders are exhausting." | "We understand your business needs" |
| **Warm but professional** | "First one's free. You've got nothing to lose." | "Risk-free, no-obligation consultation" |

### Hard rules
- First person singular ("I"), not corporate "we." The brand IS propiter.
- Speak to ONE founder, not "teams" or "organizations"
- Never: "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "leveraging," "robust," "innovative solution," "seamless," "elevate," "unlock," "game-changer"
- Specifics over adjectives: "3 days" not "fast turnaround." "Next.js + Vercel" not "modern tech stack."
- Every claim maps to something provable (research, competitor quotes, the delivered product itself)

### The voice in action
> I research your market, design a signature visual system, and ship your Next.js landing — deployed and working. Real forms. Real analytics. Real code in your GitHub repo. First one's free. If it doesn't look crafted, you've lost nothing.

## 8. Conversion Architecture

### Primary CTA
**"Tell us about your product — get your first landing free"**

This is the ONE action. It appears 4× down the page (hero, after proof, after pricing, final). Everything else is secondary/ghost.

### CTA verb
**"Tell us"** — a verb of progress, not commitment. No "Buy," no "Subscribe," no "Get started." The ask is simple: describe your product. That's it.

### Secondary flows
- **View example work** (ghost button next to primary CTA) — for skeptical visitors who need to see proof first
- **Read "How it works"** — for visitors who need the process demystified
- **FAQ scroll** — for visitors with specific objections

### Objection handling (the top 3 reasons a fit buyer wouldn't click)

| Objection | Where we disarm it | How |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **"This is just another AI wrapper, isn't it?"** | Hero subhead + How It Works section | "I'm a person. I research your market. I design with taste. This is not a prompt-to-template pipeline." The "AI Graveyard" section shows the difference visually. |
| **"What if I hate it — then I wasted money?"** | Hero trust line + Offer section | "First one's free." That's the whole answer. You literally cannot lose money. You either get a landing you love (and come back) or you walk away with a deployed site at zero cost. |
| **"What happens if I want to leave — am I locked in?"** | What You Get section + FAQ | "Your code. Your GitHub repo. Standard Next.js. Deploy it wherever you want." No proprietary editor, no platform dependency, no export gate. |

### Conversion flow
1. **Hero** — "Stop prompting an AI. Get a real landing." → primary CTA (Tell us about your product)
2. **Social proof strip** — "Built for founders who've had enough of AI templates" (testimonial snippets, once we have them; for now: the research data — competitor review quotes showing the pain is real)
3. **The AI Graveyard** — visual comparison: AI-builder output vs Landing Craft output. The problem made tangible.
4. **How It Works** — 3 steps, human-led. Demystifies the process. "You talk. I research. You launch."
5. **What You Get** — tangible checklist. Reduces anxiety about what "done" looks like.
6. **Proof** — before/after split screen (once we have projects). For now: competitor review data validating the pain. Maker credibility (propiter's GitHub).
7. **Pricing** — Free ($0) → Credits (paid) → Pro (paid). Anchor on free.
8. **FAQ** — every question is an objection. Every answer reinforces the positioning.
9. **Final CTA** — restate the core promise. One button: "Tell us about your product."

## 9. Competitive Moat

**Why this isn't easily copied:**

### Moat 1: The research phase
Most "landing services" start with a template. We start by studying your market — competitors, keywords, audience. This is the phase AI builders skip entirely, and it's the phase that makes everything else land. Templates are infinite. Market understanding is scarce.

### Moat 2: Human taste
AI produces the average of its training data — which is increasingly other AI-generated content. A human (propiter) makes intentional aesthetic decisions: a signature visual system, not the shadcn default. Taste is a non-trainable moat at scale.

### Moat 3: The guarantee
"First one's free" is powerful, but it's powerful BECAUSE the product is good. If the output were generic, the free tier would be a liability — people would try it once and leave. The moat is that the output earns repeat business.

### Moat 4: Maker brand
propiter is a real person with a GitHub. AI companies are faceless. Agencies are corporate. A maker brand builds trust with the indie hacker audience in a way neither alternative can.
